MotionRefine-R0: Reinforcing 3D Human Motion Generation
with Physical and Semantic Refinement

Anonymous Authors

Paper arXiv Code Dataset

Abstract

Recent generative models synthesize diverse 3D human motions from text, yet their outputs suffer from physical artifacts (foot skating, ground penetration) and fine-grained semantic misalignment. Existing refinement methods rely on high-variance RL estimators, expensive physics simulators, or iterative test-time optimization, and none generalizes across generator architectures.

We present MotionRefine-R0, a single-stage reinforcement learning framework that refines motions from any frozen base generator in a single forward pass. Our framework combines a lightweight residual policy with fully differentiable composite rewards (physical plausibility + semantic alignment + reconstruction fidelity), enabling stable low-variance policy gradient updates without multi-step rollouts or physics simulators.

Without any generator-specific modification, a single lightweight policy adding only 2 ms inference overhead refines four structurally different generators: MoMask (263D discrete VQ), MDM (263D continuous diffusion), MLD (263D latent diffusion), and HY-Motion (201D SMPL-H), achieving 64.3%, 84.0%, 68.6%, and 35.2% MPJPE reduction respectively, with 74.0% foot skating elimination on MoMask and 96.0% on MDM, all while maintaining FID below 1.0.
